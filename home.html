About Our System

A health monitoring system that measures BPM (beats per minute), temperature, and SPo2 (peripheral oxygen saturation) is a useful tool for tracking a person's overall health status.

BPM is an important measure of cardiovascular health and can help detect irregular heart rhythms, such as atrial fibrillation, which can increase the risk of stroke. Monitoring BPM can also provide early warning signs of heart disease and help people maintain a healthy heart rate during exercise.

Temperature monitoring is a common method used to detect fever, which can be a sign of infection or illness. A sudden increase in body temperature can indicate an infection, while a decrease in body temperature can be a sign of hypothermia.

SPo2 monitoring is a non-invasive way of measuring the amount of oxygen in the blood. Low levels of oxygen can indicate respiratory problems such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), or pneumonia. Monitoring SPo2 levels can help healthcare providers detect potential breathing problems and adjust treatment accordingly.
